3. The PBC cannot control the Foundation. The Foundation cannot distribute profits to the PBC. This separation is load-bearing.
4. Separate boards. Separate EINs. Arm's-length contracts. No ownership or control relationship.
5. Stated public benefit: supporting charitable giving through technology.
6. Iowa requires no charitable solicitation registration. Accept donations immediately.
7. Tax-deductible status pending 501(c)(3) approval — donors notified upon approval.
8. File the PBC first — this is your operating entity, bank account, and contract signatory.
9. The Foundation never sells content rights — that would jeopardize tax-exempt status.
10. The PBC owns the app, all technology, the Glimpse brand, and all content produced from campaigns.
11. Donor dollars flow 100% to recipients. Operational costs are covered by campaign sponsors.
12. These entities operate independently. This separation is load-bearing for both tax-exempt status and regulatory compliance.

## SECTION 3 — TOKEN MODEL (25–34)

25. This creates fundamental buying pressure backed by real revenue — not speculation.
26. Buybacks are funded by real PBC revenue from city partner contracts — not speculation. Fundamental support tied to business performance, not hype.
27. Each donor receives a proportional share based on their share of total donations. Allocation resets each period — new campaign, new competition.
28. Token holders — not the founding team — decide how the buyback pool is deployed.
29. Whale donors are a feature, not a bug. Large donations drive up campaign volume — more impact for the recipient.
30. Large donations create competition — other donors are incentivized to give more to capture share.
31. No prior connection between donor and recipient is permitted. The vetting process is the anti-gaming layer for the entire token mechanic.
32. Tokens are earned retroactively based on participation — not promised upfront as an investment.
33. PBC revenue grows → more buybacks. Airdrop worth more → donors compete harder. Campaign volume grows → more impact → more press → more city partners.
34. The first governance vote occurs June 1, 2027, when the entire supply unlocks.
