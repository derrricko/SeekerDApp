## 1. MARKET SIZE & TOTAL GIVING

- U.S. charitable giving reached **$592.5 billion** in 2024, a 6.3% increase from 2023 — a new all-time high in current dollars *(Giving USA 2025, Indiana University Lilly Family School of Philanthropy)*
- Global charitable giving market estimated at **$530 billion** in 2024, projected to reach **$848 billion by 2033** at 6% CAGR *(Business Research Insights, 2025)*
- Individuals gave **$392.45 billion**, representing **66% of all charitable giving** — the largest donor segment *(Giving USA 2025)*
- Corporate giving rose to **$44.40 billion** in 2024, a 9.1% increase — a new record *(Giving USA 2025)*
- Foundation giving exceeded **$100 billion for the third straight year**, reaching $109.81 billion *(Giving USA 2025)*
- Charity fundraising platform market valued at **$1.09 billion** in 2024, projected to reach **$2.51 billion by 2033** at 9.1% CAGR *(Business Research Insights, 2025)*

## 2. DONOR RETENTION — OVERALL

- Overall donor retention rate: **26.3%** as of Q2 2025 — nearly 3 out of 4 donors don't come back *(Fundraising Effectiveness Project Q2 2025, AFP/GivingTuesday)*
- Retention has declined slightly from 26.4% in Q2 2024 to 26.3% in Q2 2025 *(FEP Q2 2025 via Bloomerang)*
- For North American nonprofits, overall annual donor retention dropped to **46.6%** in 2023 *(Dataro 2024 Donor Retention Analysis)*
- **Four consecutive years** of declining retention rates through Q3 2024 *(FEP Q3 2024 Report, AFP)*
- Industry benchmarks show annual donor retention hovers around **50%**, meaning half the donor base must be replaced every year *(Virtuous 2025 Nonprofit Benchmark Report)*
- Top-performing nonprofits achieve **67.7% retention** through active relationship-building *(Virtuous 2025 Nonprofit Benchmark Report)*

---

## 3. DONOR RETENTION — NEW DONORS

- New donor retention rate: **7.2%** in Q1 2024 — a 7.6% drop year-over-year *(Kindsight/FEP Q1 2024)*
- New donor retention held steady at **11.0%** year-to-date as of Q2 2025 *(FEP Q2 2025 via Bloomerang)*
- **~70% of donors give only once** to an organization and never return *(Kindsight/FEP Q1 2024)*
- Only **28% of new donors** gave a second gift in 2023, projected to drop to 26.9% in 2024 *(Dataro 2024 Donor Retention Analysis)*
- ~23% of donors churn within **six months** of their first donation *(Kindsight/FEP Q1 2024)*
- 10.7% drop in new donors and 10.2% decrease in new retained donors through mid-2025 *(FEP Q2 2025 via NonProfit PRO)*

---

## 4. DONOR RETENTION — REPEAT & FREQUENCY

- Repeat donor retention: **36.3%** year-to-date — dramatically higher than new donor retention at 11% *(FEP Q2 2025 via Bloomerang)*
- Once a donor gives a second gift ("the golden donation"), retention rates nearly **double to 59%** *(Dataro 2024 Donor Retention Analysis)*
- One-time donor retention: **13.9%** *(FEP Q2 2025 via Bloomerang)*
- Two-time donor retention: **30.0%** *(FEP Q2 2025 via Bloomerang)*
- Three-to-six-time donor retention: **55.3%** *(FEP Q2 2025 via Bloomerang)*
- Seven-plus-time donor retention: **86.2%** *(FEP Q2 2025 via Bloomerang)*
- Repeat donors now account for more than **60% of total fundraising dollars** *(FEP Q2 2025 via NonProfit PRO)*
- Increasing retention by just **5% for two years** could lead to **20% revenue growth** over five years *(Virtuous 2025 Nonprofit Benchmark Report)*

## 5. SMALL DONOR DECLINE

- Micro donors ($1-$100) make up **57% of all donors** but saw a **10.5% drop** in giving and 10.4% decline in participation *(FEP Q2 2025 via NonProfit PRO)*
- Small-dollar donors ($1-$100) experienced a **12.4%** year-over-year decrease in Q3 2024 *(FEP Q3 2024 via NonProfit PRO)*
- Small donors contribute only **1.8% of total dollars** but are responsible for two-thirds of the loss in donor numbers *(FEP Q3 2024 via NonProfit PRO)*
- New donors had an **11.5%** year-over-year drop; they comprise 37.5% of donors, making lost acquisition a major driver of small-donor decline *(FEP Q3 2024 via NonProfit PRO)*

---

## 6. STRUCTURAL VULNERABILITY — MEGA-DONOR DEPENDENCE

- Supersize donors ($50K+) account for **~50% of all dollars raised** despite being a tiny fraction of donors *(FEP Q2 2025 via NonProfit PRO)*
- Supersize donor performance was **down 7.7%** over midyear 2024 *(FEP Q2 2025 via NonProfit PRO)*
- The sector is raising **more money from fewer people** — an unsustainable model *(FEP Q2 2025 via NonProfit PRO)*
- 80% of an organization's funding typically comes from **20% of its donors** (Pareto Principle) *(Event Smart, 2024)*

## 7. COST OF FUNDRAISING & OVERHEAD

- Acquiring a new donor costs **5x more** than retaining a current one *(Dataro 2024 Donor Retention Analysis)*
- Nonprofits typically have overhead ratios of around **20%** — 1 out of every 5 dollars goes to fundraising, admin, and operations *(Giving Compass, 2023)*
- Watchdogs recommend nonprofits spend no more than **35%** of donations on overhead *(BoardEffect / BBB Wise Giving Alliance)*
- Charity Navigator gives best ratings to orgs spending less than **$0.10 to raise $1** *(Neon One, 2025)*
- CharityWatch considers a charity highly efficient with a Program % of **75% or greater** and Cost to Raise $100 of **$25 or less** *(CharityWatch)*
- Fundraising platforms typically charge **3% to 10%** in fees *(Zeffy, 2024)*
- Credit card companies and banks charge up to **3-5%** per transaction on donations *(Cancer Research Institute, 2025)*
- Average lifetime value of a monthly donor: **$7,604** *(4aGoodCause, 2025)*
- Average recurring monthly donation: **$24** per M+R Benchmarks *(4aGoodCause, 2025)*

## 8. DONOR DEMOGRAPHICS & BEHAVIOR

- Average charitable donor in the U.S. is **64 years old** and makes **two charitable donations per year** *(NPTrust, updated Feb 2026)*
- **63% of donors prefer to give online** with credit or debit card *(NPTrust, updated Feb 2026)*
- **91% of high net worth households** give to charity *(Nonprofits Source, 2024)*
- Average high net worth donation: **$29,269** in 2023 *(Nonprofits Source, 2024)*
- **31% of donors worldwide** give to charities outside their country of residence *(Nonprofits Source, 2024)*
- Only **24% of Americans have a will** in 2025, down from 33% three years ago — limiting bequest giving *(BWF / Giving USA 2025)*
- Donor-advised funds maintained **$251 billion in assets** in 2024 *(BWF / Giving USA 2025)*

## 9. CRYPTO PHILANTHROPY

- Over **$1 billion** in cryptocurrency donated to nonprofits in 2024 — a record year *(The Giving Block, 2025 Annual Report on Crypto Philanthropy)*
- Crypto donations projected to reach **$2.5 billion in 2025**, more than doubling 2024 *(The Giving Block via Giving Compass)*
- Fidelity Charitable received **$688 million** in crypto donations through Nov 2024, up from $49 million in all of 2023 *(CNBC / Fidelity Charitable, Dec 2024)*
- In the first four months of 2024 alone, crypto contributions to Fidelity Charitable reached **$300 million** — nearly equal to all of 2021 *(Fidelity Charitable)*
- **70% of Forbes' Top 100 U.S. charities** now accept crypto donations, up from 12% in 2020 *(The Giving Block / Infinite Giving)*
- **95% of surveyed crypto owners** made charitable contributions in 2021 *(Fidelity Charitable)*
- Bitcoin donors contributed **64% of all known crypto donations** in 2024 *(The Giving Block, 2025 Annual Report)*
- Average crypto donation size in 2024 was above **$10,000** *(Infinite Giving)*
- Solana surged **over 100%** in 2024, outperforming many altcoins *(The Giving Block, 2025 Annual Report)*
- Stablecoins processed nearly **$30 trillion** in transactions in 2024, surpassing Visa ($11T) and Mastercard ($2T) combined *(The Giving Block, 2025 Annual Report)*
- Crypto transaction fees can be as low as **a fraction of a percent**, vs 3-5% for credit cards *(Cancer Research Institute, 2025)*
- ~61% of crypto users are men, 36% women; largest income range is $50K-$100K (29% of users) *(Infinite Giving, 2025)*
- Part of the **$84.4 trillion** generational wealth transfer from boomers to younger people *(The Giving Block via Giving Compass)*

## 10. WHY DONORS LEAVE (THE GLIMPSE OPPORTUNITY)

- #1 reason for donor churn: **lack of connection and transparency** — donors don't see their impact *(Dataro / Bloomerang / FEP aggregate findings)*
- Donors who feel **personally connected** and see how donations make a difference are dramatically more likely to give again *(Kindsight, 2025 / Virtuous 2025)*
- Personalizing donor experience and clearly showing impact are the top recommended retention strategies *(Giving USA 2024 / Bloomerang / Virtuous)*
- "Nonprofits that focus on receiving that golden donation naturally increase their average donor retention rate about threefold" *(FEP Q2 2025 via Bloomerang)*
- Reactivation rate for lapsed donors is as little as **4%** and steadily declining *(Dataro / FEP 2024)*
- Only **9.8% of lapsed donors** are reactivated annually on average *(Dataro 2024 Donor Retention Analysis)*
